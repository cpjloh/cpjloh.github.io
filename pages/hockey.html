Autonomous Hockey Table

March 2019

This autonomous hockey table was part of a project by a previous team. Thus, my team used the existing hardware and rewrote all the software. This rewrite included improved vision capabilities, faster PID control, and reduced noise. This was an opportunity for me to use Python and OpenCV.

I was the sole author of the computer vision used in this project. The algorithm begins begins by thresholding the image to remove all pixels that are outside of a red color bounds. Next, the erode morphological transformation is applied to reduce the noise in the image and remove the red on the air hockey table. The table then selects the largest section of red on the table and makes a prediction of its future positions by using a history of its previous positions to approximate the puck's velocity.

There are two issues that impacted the project's final result. First, the encoder values began to drift. This results in the slider confidentally moving to hit a puck but missing as the positioning system has lost accuracy. This could be improved by recalibrating the slider every few seconds using a position found using the camera. The second issue was the table's ability to play at very high speeds, which is the result of the puck blurring and becoming invisible to the algorithm. Our team wished to use a better camera, but unfortunately we did not have enough time.

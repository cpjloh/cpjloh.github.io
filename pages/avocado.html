AVOCADO (Autonomous Vehicles Operating Collaboratively And Detecting Obstacles)

October 2018 - June 2019

My computer engineering capstone project aims to implement lane keeping, object detection and classification, and vehicle-to-vehicle communication to allow a platoon of vehicles to collaboratively respond to a dynamic roadway.

My primary contributions are:

  • Lane tracking using computer vision
  • Project management
  • Communication between mechanical and computer engineering teams

My technical work has been utilizing OpenCV with Python for lane tracking. Some videos of my work are below.

Note: I have really enjoyed this project, as well as my computer vision course, and will ideally find work in this field.

The above video shows my lane detection algorithm on a section of southbound Highway 101, north of Paso Robles. The algorithm identifies the lane lines within a region of interest and paints them green for programmer visibility. The center point tracks the center of the lane, which is used as input to our PID lane keeping controller.

The following video was taken on February 16, 2019 after many hours of my teammate and me working on electronics and software. The video shows lane lines mounted on a chair (seen at the bottom of the frame) and a camera (on the desk). The camera captures video, which it feeds to the not-so-micro Jetson TX2 microcontroller for the necessary image processing. Finally, a pwm signal is sent to an Arduino (we do this because the Arduino, unlike the Jetson, has pwm hardware), which then controls the vehicle.
